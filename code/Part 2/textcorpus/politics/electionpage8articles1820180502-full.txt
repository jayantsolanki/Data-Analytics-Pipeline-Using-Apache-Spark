MOSCOW  Armenia's parliament will hold another vote for the post of prime minister on May 8, its press service said on Wednesday, after a vote on Tuesday failed to secure a majority for opposition leader Nikol Pashinyan.Pashinyan was the only candidate in the election, which followed weeks of protests in the country's capital Yerevan. He has since called for a nationwide campaign of civil disobedience. (Reporting by Hasmik Mkrtchyan; Writing by Polina Ivanova; Editing by Christian Lowe)
